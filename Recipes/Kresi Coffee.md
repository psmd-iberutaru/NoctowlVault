### Information
There really isn't a known name for this style of brewing coffee. It seems to be a variation on Turkish coffee. But, it is a how a very specific Croatian makes their coffee.

**Serving Size:** 1
#### Source
This recipe was obtained from Krešimir Baričević.

# Ingredients
Main:
- 1 cup: Water, boiled
- 2 teaspoons: Coffee powder
- 1 tablespoons: Milk
Whiteners (Optional):
- 1 teaspoon: Sugar

**Ingredient Considerations:**
- None

# Instructions
1. Boil water in a kettle or equivalent.
2. Add the coffee powder and (optional) sugar into a cezve. Then add 1 cup of boiling water into the cezve, briefly stir mixture until the coffee powder is in suspension.
3. Re-boil the coffee mixture in the cezve until it lightly foams.
4. Pour the mixture into a standard coffee mug. Add milk into the coffee mug and briefly stir.

**Instruction Considerations:**
- Before drinking, the coffee can be set aside to cool down and have the coffee grounds stratify and settle. This provides for a “cleaner” coffee beverage.
- A black version may also be made; omit the milk and sugar.
