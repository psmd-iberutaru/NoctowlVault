### Information
Rice with a nice topping which goes well with nori.

**Serving Size:** 30-45
#### Source
This recipe was obtained from the “[Hawaii's Best Pupu & Potluck](https://worldcat.org/en/title/62615598)” cookbook.

# Ingredients
Main:
- 3 cup: Rice, raw.
- 9: Shiitake mushrooms, dried.
- 8 ounces: Imitation crab, shredded or chopped.
- 8 ounces: Sour cream
- 1 cup: Mayonnaise
- 1/2 cup: Furikake
Nori:
- 1.6 ounces: Tamka yaki nori

**Ingredient Considerations:**
- Temaki yaki nori often comes in packages of 10 sheets, weighing a total of 0.4 oz. Buying 4 packages is equivalent to the recipe requirements. Alternative nori may be substituted.

# Instructions
1. Wash and cook the 3 cups of rice. A rice cooker is preferred.
2. Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in water to reconstitute them. Discard any mushroom stems. After 15-20 minutes, remove the soaked shiitake mushrooms, squeezing out any excess water. Dice the mushrooms.
3. Mix diced shiitake mushrooms, imitation crab, sour cream, and mayonnaise in a bowl.
4. In a 9x13-inch pan, spread the cooked rice into a layer. Spread the furikake over the rice. Then, spread the crab mixture (from Step 3) over the furikake covered rice.
5. Broil the pan for 8-10 minutes or until the top is appropriately browned.
6. Cut into squares in the pan, serve with the nori.

**Instruction Considerations:**
- Reconstitution time of shiitake mushrooms may vary, ensure that the mushrooms are fully reconstituted before continuing.
- You may add additional ingredients and layer them between the rice to create a more layered dish.
